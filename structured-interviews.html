Structured Interviews

18 Jun 2021 · Work

Interviews are the gateway into a company. The wrong selection of a candidate is a loss to him and the company. The candidate loses an opportunity to do something he would have been good at, eventually bringing him back to square one at the end of the experience — and the company loses the resources it employed to train a candidate who was not suited for the job at hand.

Realising this, we took up the task of imbibing the culture of structured interviews into the company. After a study of all the interviewing techniques, we came across the interviewing procedure followed by Google. We used that, together with the techniques followed by the US Embassy, to create our own version of structured interviews.

A few critical points for the interviewer:

  1. The job description: every position we try to fill needs a clear job description — it helps the candidate know what he is interviewing for and lets him plan his objectives for the future.
  2. Review the resume and self-reflection document well.
  3. Select competencies with respect to the job description written.
  4. Frame good questions and probe questions, to allow the candidate to express himself.
  5. Revisit great rejected candidates: some candidates may not fit the job they interviewed for but would fit perfectly in another department in the organisation.
"Hiring is the most important people function you have, and most of us aren't as good at it as we think. Refocusing your resources on hiring better will have a higher return than almost any training program you can develop."
